Precision and recall for retrieval are fundamental metrics in **RAG systems, search engines, and vector databases**, because they measure how well your retriever is finding the *right context before the LLM even generates an answer*.

A senior framing:

> Retrieval metrics measure whether your system is bringing back the **right information (precision)** and **enough of the right information (recall)**.

---

# 1. Basic idea in retrieval

For a query, your retriever returns a set of documents/chunks.

You compare it to a **ground-truth relevant set**.

---

## Key sets

* **Retrieved set (R)** → what your system returned
* **Relevant set (G)** → what *should* have been returned

---

# 2. Precision

## 2.1 Definition

> Of everything retrieved, how much is actually relevant?

\text{Precision} = \frac{|R \cap G|}{|R|}

---

## 2.2 Intuition

Precision answers:

> “Did I return mostly correct context, or a lot of noise?”

---

## 2.3 Example

Retrieved 5 chunks:

* 3 are relevant
* 2 are irrelevant

```text id="p1"
Precision = 3/5 = 0.6
```

---

## 2.4 High precision means

✔ low noise
✔ better LLM focus
✔ reduced hallucination risk

---

## 2.5 Low precision means

❌ irrelevant chunks
❌ confused LLM
❌ wasted context window

---

# 3. Recall

## 3.1 Definition

> Of all relevant documents, how many did we retrieve?

\text{Recall} = \frac{|R \cap G|}{|G|}

---

## 3.2 Intuition

Recall answers:

> “Did I retrieve enough of the important information?”

---

## 3.3 Example

Relevant chunks = 10
Retrieved relevant chunks = 7

```text id="r1"
Recall = 7/10 = 0.7
```

---

## 3.4 High recall means

✔ system is unlikely to miss key facts
✔ good coverage of knowledge

---

## 3.5 Low recall means

❌ missing important context
❌ LLM hallucination risk increases
❌ incomplete answers

---

# 4. Precision vs Recall tradeoff

This is the most important concept.

| Strategy         | Effect                         |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------ |
| increase top-k   | higher recall, lower precision |
| strict filtering | higher precision, lower recall |

---

## Intuition

* Precision = “quality of retrieved results”
* Recall = “coverage of retrieved results”

---

# 5. Why this matters in RAG systems

In RAG:

```text id="rag1"
Query → Retriever → LLM → Answer
```

If retrieval is bad:

* high precision → LLM gets clean but possibly incomplete context
* high recall → LLM gets everything but noisy context

---

# 6. Top-k retrieval impact

If you set:

* k = 3 → high precision, low recall
* k = 20 → high recall, lower precision

---

# 7. Precision/Recall in vector search

Used to evaluate:

* embeddings quality
* ANN index performance (FAISS, HNSW)
* rerankers

---

# 8. Recall@k (most used in production)

Instead of full recall:

\text{Recall@k} = \frac{\text{relevant docs in top-k}}{\text{total relevant docs}}

---

## Example

* relevant docs = 10
* top-5 retrieved contains 6 relevant docs (impossible, so capped at 5)

```text id="rk1"
Recall@5 = 4/10 = 0.4
```

---

# 9. Precision@k

\text{Precision@k} = \frac{\text{relevant docs in top-k}}{k}

---

## Example

Top-5 results:

* 3 relevant
* 2 irrelevant

```text id="pk1"
Precision@5 = 3/5 = 0.6
```

---

# 10. Real-world interpretation

| Metric    | Meaning in RAG               |
| --------- | ---------------------------- |
| Precision | “Is my context clean?”       |
| Recall    | “Did I miss important info?” |

---

# 11. Tradeoff in LLM systems

---

## High precision systems

* reranked top-k
* strict filtering
* good for customer support bots

✔ safe answers
❌ may miss rare facts

---

## High recall systems

* larger top-k
* broader retrieval
* good for research assistants

✔ complete answers
❌ noisy context

---

# 12. How production systems improve both

---

## 12.1 Hybrid search

* BM25 + vector search

---

## 12.2 Reranking

* first retrieve high recall
* then filter for precision

---

## 12.3 Chunk optimization

* better chunking improves both metrics

---

## 12.4 Query rewriting

* improves recall by expanding query intent

---

# 13. Evaluation pipeline

```text id="eval1"
Query
 ↓
Retriever
 ↓
Top-k results
 ↓
Compare with ground truth
 ↓
Compute precision/recall metrics
```

---

# 14. Common mistakes

❌ evaluating only LLM output (not retrieval)
❌ ignoring recall (very dangerous in RAG)
❌ using wrong ground truth definition
❌ not separating retrieval vs generation errors

---

# 15. Key insight

> Retrieval quality sets the upper bound of LLM performance.

Even the best LLM cannot fix poor recall.

---

# 16. Senior-level interview answer

Precision and recall in retrieval systems measure the quality of retrieved documents relative to a ground-truth relevant set. Precision is the fraction of retrieved documents that are relevant, while recall is the fraction of relevant documents that are successfully retrieved.

In RAG and search systems, these metrics are often computed at top-k (Precision@k and Recall@k) to evaluate ranking performance. Precision reflects noise in retrieved context, while recall measures coverage of relevant information. A key tradeoff exists between the two, and production systems typically use hybrid search, reranking, and query expansion techniques to balance them effectively.
